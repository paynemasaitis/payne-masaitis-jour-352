Baltimore Orioles Team Trend Report

By Payne Masaitis | April 21, 2026

Baltimore Orioles see steady dip in strikeout rate and batting average to start 2026

Orioles shortstop Gunnar Henderson waits on deck during a game against the Arizona Diamondbacks (Photo credits: Patrick Smith/Getty Images)

The Baltimore Orioles offense has been unchararcteristically sluggish to begin the 2026 season. Through the team's first 23 games, they have averaged a whopping 9.74 strikeouts per game. That is more than one whiff per inning. Baltimore shortstop Gunnar Henderson, a former All-Star, leads the team in that category at 33 punch outs. Directly behind Henderson is Baltimore's $155 million first baseman Pete Alonso, who has whiffed 29 times in 23 games. As a whole, the Orioles are recording nearly 1.3 more strikeouts per game than the 2023 season. It simply has not been an ideal start for the Orioles' most premium hitters in 2026, especially after an offseason of immense hype in Charm City.

Strikeouts are not the only downfall of Baltimore's miserable 2026 offense. The team has also struggled to be efficient as a whole. Baltimore's season batting average through 23 games of 2026 is almost a full 0.01 points down from the 2025 season, continuing a concerning downward spiral from the plate. The Orioles' batting average has plummeted from an impressive .255 in their 101-win 2023 season to an underwhelming .226 in the early stages of the 2026 campaign. As of April 21, Baltimore possesses five starting batters that are batting under .200, a statistic that makes their 10 wins this season a mystery. Henderson and Alonso are included in that bunch, aiming to escape the grasp of a nasty slump as the Orioles take on the Kansas City Royals on Tuesday night.
