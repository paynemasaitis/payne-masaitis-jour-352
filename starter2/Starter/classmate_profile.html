Meet Rachel Lawrence

Sophomore at the University of Maryland, JOUR352

Rachel Lawrence, a sophomore from New Jersey, traced her interest in pursuing a journalism career back to her family’s consumption of daily news.

“I’ve always seen the news as family time,” she said. “It was just something that was always on around the house.”

Lawrence, who grew up just 20 minutes outside of Philadelphia, was drawn to sports as a child, pushing her to eventually pursue a career in sports journalism.

Before that, however, the sophomore had career plans of being a sports statistician, but quickly pivoted.

“I realized I hated calculus,” Lawrence said with a laugh.

Outside of journalism, Lawrence is a waitress at a bowling alley, a job that she worked over the University of Maryland’s winter break. “There were a lot of interesting customers,” she said.

If Lawrence could have any superpower in the world, she would choose Batman’s wealth and gadgets over any supernatural ability.

“I just want to be able to have his money,” Lawrence joked.

She listed flying as a close second to Batman’s catalog.

When asked about a vegetable that she absolutely despised, Lawrence did not hesitate to list Brussels sprouts, to the dismay of other group members.

“I can’t stand Brussels sprouts,” she said with distaste.
